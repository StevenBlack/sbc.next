Career Highlights

Since 2007 and ongoing: Contributor to a variety of open-source projects including Bootstrap, jQuery Mobile, jQuery UI and others. Here's my public activity on Github since 2009.

Since 2005 and ongoing: Continuing consulting relationships with MetalCraft Marine (Kingston, on, Canada) on the strategic use of the web for international industrial sales of specialty fireboats and patrol boats.

Since 1999 and ongoing: Creator, host, and editor-in-chief of the acclaimed FoxPro Wiki which is an interactive knowledgebase for Visual FoxPro developers. 

Since 2005 and ongoing: Ownership and management of K7Waterfront, a local Kingston Ontario waterfront watchdog. Also creation and upkeep of the Kingston Taxpayers Association website.

Since 1993 and ongoing: Creation, launch, and refinement of Steven Black's INTL Toolkit, an acclaimed tool that serves as a framework and guide for internationalizing FoxPro applications.

2005-2010: Consulting relationships with a variety of clients including, most notably, Mellowood Medical (Toronto, on, Canada), DAA Northwest Inc (Spokane wa), Waudware (Toronto, on, Canada), and 2020 IT Solutions (Saskatoon, sk, Canada).

1993-2010: Over 45 appearances as a speaker and panel member at Microsoft-sponsored developer conferences in North America and Europe. Co-author three books on FoxPro and Visual FoxPro, and acknowledged in several others.  Published over a dozen articles in software journals in North America and Europe.

2003-2004: Project architect for Net Learning, in Knoxville, tn. Net Learning is a leader in the field of on-line computer based learning, and learning management systems, for the medical and nuclear power industries.

2000-2002: Chief IS architect, eMedicine.com, in Syracuse NY. Among other things, eMedicine is the largest public on-line peer-reviewed medical repository on the Internet. eMedicine has since been purchased by WebMD and, as of 2010, most of the systems I first designed for eMedicine are still in-use today by WebMD.

1997-2001: Consulting relationships in the usa with 3M (ct), the Qualcomm Corporation (ca), Johnson & Johnson (nj),  Daimler Chrysler (ny), Investlink (ny), Flash Creative Management (nj), and in Europe with Nokia (Lausane), The Red Cross (Geneva) and GSI (Munich).

1996: Development team technical-lead for Philip Morris eema in Budapest, Hungary, creating sms: A multi-country, multi-language sales, distribution, marketing and logistics system for Marlboro brands in Eastern Europe. About this, Marlboro continues to be the top selling brand of any kind in the world.

1994-95: Development team leader for Philip Morris sa in Istanbul Turkey, creating sds: A multi-country, multi-language sales and distribution logistics system for Marlboro brands in Turkey and Eastern Europe.

1993-95: Consulting with Micromega Systems of San Francisco, ca, for the internationalization of FoxFire!, the leading third-party reporting tool for Visual FoxPro.

1993-95: Consulting relationship with its Inc., of Winston-Salem, nc, for modernization and internationalization of their suite of products for logistics planning and dispatching used by the trucking industry worldwide. Features of the system include satellite tracking and communications for real-time vehicle positioning and messaging. its is now a division of the Qualcomm corporation.

1992-93: Head developer of cts, a commodity transportation, dispatching, and tracking system used by the unhcr in Geneva to plan, distribute, and track relief aid in the Former Yugoslavia. cts has since evolved to be integrated into unhcr operations worldwide, and is today a de-facto standard for disaster relief software in the field.

1987-92: Project leader for North Sails Inc., of Milford ct. Responsible for the design, development, and implementation of corporate information systems in North America and Europe.

1986-87: Member of the win Exports development team, a project for the Canadian Department of External Affairs, which saw the computerization of all Canada’s embassies and trade consulates worldwide. Responsible for the multilingual and the reporting functions in the system.
